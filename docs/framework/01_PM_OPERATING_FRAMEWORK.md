# PM OPERATING FRAMEWORK — V1

## The Core Question

Before writing tickets, ask:

What is the user trying to accomplish, inside which system, with which permissions, and what must be true for engineering to build it correctly?

## The PM Flow

1. Clarify the business goal.
2. Identify the actors.
3. Map the user journey.
4. Identify systems involved.
5. Define use cases.
6. Make product decisions explicit.
7. Write user stories.
8. Add acceptance criteria.
9. Identify dependencies.
10. Identify risks and edge cases.
11. Define what is in scope and out of scope.
12. Prepare engineering handoff.

## The Universal PM Questions

### Business

- What business outcome are we trying to create?
- Who benefits if this works?
- What does success look like?
- Is this a revenue, retention, efficiency, compliance, or user-experience problem?

### Users

- Who is the primary user?
- Who is the secondary user?
- Who approves or controls access?
- Who receives the final output?
- Who can be harmed by a wrong implementation?

### Systems

- Where does the user start?
- Which system owns authentication?
- Which system owns the data?
- Which system owns the output?
- Which system sends, stores, or tracks the result?

### Permissions

- Who can see what?
- Who can create what?
- Who can edit what?
- Who can approve what?
- Who can send or publish what?

### Product Decisions

- Is the experience white-label, partner-branded, or client-branded?
- Is SSO required?
- Is approval required before sending or publishing?
- Which system is the source of truth?
- Which service generates the output?
- What is MVP and what is V2?

### Engineering Handoff

- What should engineering build first?
- What should not be built yet?
- What are the acceptance criteria?
- What are the dependencies?
- What are the open questions?
- What needs product/design approval before implementation?
