## Newsletter Draft

# AI Compliance Brief: Practical Readiness Steps for the Next Quarter

Hello,

As AI-assisted workflows become more common across customer operations, compliance teams are under pressure to move from high-level policy discussions to practical execution.

This week's brief focuses on three operational priorities that teams can act on now.

## 1. Start with an AI System Inventory

The first step is to understand where AI is already being used across the organization.

Teams should document:

- which AI systems are in use;
- which business process each system supports;
- who owns each system;
- what data each system uses;
- whether the system affects customers, employees, or regulated decisions.

A clear inventory gives compliance, product, and engineering teams a shared starting point.

## 2. Define Human Review for AI-Assisted Communication

AI-assisted content can improve speed, but it should not remove human responsibility.

For customer-facing communication, teams should define:

- which content sources the AI system is allowed to use;
- who reviews generated drafts;
- which claims require additional approval;
- when content should be escalated to legal or compliance;
- how final approval is recorded.

The goal is not to block useful automation.

The goal is to make the workflow safe, reviewable, and accountable.

## 3. Embed Compliance Controls into Existing Tools

Compliance processes are more likely to work when they fit naturally into existing team workflows.

Instead of forcing users into separate systems, teams should consider:

- embedded review steps;
- clear approval states;
- source references;
- lightweight audit logs;
- simple export or publishing controls.

This keeps the process practical while preserving governance.

## Recommended Next Step

Review one AI-assisted customer communication workflow this quarter and answer three questions:

1. What source content is the system allowed to use?
2. Who approves the final output?
3. What event is logged when the content is approved or exported?

These three answers can help turn AI governance from policy into operational practice.

## PM Interpretation

This output demonstrates why the upstream PM work matters.

Without clear product decisions, the final output could create serious ambiguity:

- Which content was allowed?
- Who approved the newsletter?
- Was the newsletter sent or only exported?
- Did ComplianceBrief store recipient data?
- Which CRM segment was selected?
- Can the generated text be traced back to approved sources?

The PM framework ensures these questions are answered before engineering builds the workflow.

## PM Lesson

The final output is not just a piece of content.

It is the result of product decisions about:

- permissions;
- content ownership;
- data ownership;
- human approval;
- source traceability;
- export boundaries;
- audit logging.

A good PM works backward from the desired user outcome and makes the invisible product rules explicit.
